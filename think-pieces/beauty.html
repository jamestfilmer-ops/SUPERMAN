Beauty

Jul 5, 2022  ·  Loading...

"Beauty awakens the soul to act." — Dante Alighieri

Some people sit stranded, wondering where they should go in this life. When you are deep in sorrow, eating from the waste bin, begging for coins, on your death bed, addicted to the poison that was once your choice and is now your burden, when your loved ones have died, when you have lost everything — these are the times when you must chase Beauty, an elusive and fascinating creature. It may seem odd, even patronizing, or tone-deaf to suggest that a man without a roof go to an art museum or venture to the outskirts of town to the local creek bed just when the Sun dips into the river.

I believe in the power of the beautiful, and I think that if one has life, there is a remnant of the Beauty within us all. Beauty is how we measure our proximity to Death. The truly beautiful things stave off Death for an unimaginable length of time — mountainsides, sunsets, paintings, literature, poetry, and humans. Though ultimately temporary, there is an essence of the eternal left within them, or at least we perceive it as so.

Contrarily, when Beauty is gone and the world is dark, soiled, foggy, and frightening, Death has some time at the door, standing at the threshold, swinging his watch. He is rather rude. He criticizes you, your work, and your life.

"What have you amounted to? A Hell of a legacy you left here. You might as well forget yourself. Look at you; you are worthless." And so on, and so forth.

At this point, we find ourselves begging, scrapping, weeping, hopeless. What is worse? We may put ourselves in those positions of despair. Addiction, anger, jealousy, and vice can draw us away from Beauty, and we too often chase after these vices like a dog chases a car. Death would like us to sit in the past, always. Beauty draws on the power of the future, the everlasting, the idea that things — paintings, words, music, beachside sunrises, pictures — outlive us.

· · ·

I cannot emphasize enough how important it is for a person to create. The reasons are plenty. First, it forces you to confront the idea that you are worthless. If you can create something to which you can attach meaning, you have inevitably given yourself meaning, and thus you are not worthless. Second, it makes you believe beyond yourself. You can ask yourself questions such as, "What is the world like? Why is this so? How can I make this better?" I am not expecting people to become the next Van Gogh or Michelangelo. However, everything living has the capacity for Beauty, so I know you have something to offer.
